# Sound treatment in a room

$$------------$$

## Goal

Trying to improve the sound properties or whatever it is for a room where a soprano lyric singer practices.

The range claimed by the singer is from a A3 to a F5. So 220Hz to 698Hz in the fundamental frequency.

The original setup is a room of 270x410 with a sloped roof from 230 to 250cm in height.
The piano and singer face one of the short walls when singing.

## Trials

### First try (not an improvement)

Using
two [polyurethane foam panels of 120x200x4cm](https://www.leroymerlin.es/productos/1-ud-de-panel-poliuretano-geopannel-multiaislante-espesor-40mm-2x1-2m-r-1-143-94809128.html).

This panels are placed in the back of the piano and the singer, in the short walls and the singer did not feel an
improvement in the room.

### Second try (a useful improvement)

Using
4x [compacted PET fiber panels of 120x60x2.5cm](https://perfectacoustic.es/producto/bilnora-extra-denso-paneles-120x60x25cm/).

The singer claimed the room was much better. Could not explain why but she said she could sing better because she could
hear herself better.

This panels claim an NRC of 0.8 peaking ata factor of 1 around 1kHz so maybe removing the extra frequencies other than
the fundamental makes it more clear where to land the notes?
